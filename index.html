About

Builder by nature,
leader by choice.

I'm a sophomore at Dougherty Valley High School in San Ramon. Since building my first robot from metal parts and a Raspberry Pi during the pandemic in 5th grade, I've been driven by one question: how do machines learn to move through the world?


I lead VEX Robotics Team 3303J for over six years — designing, CAD-modeling, building, and programming. This season: States, Nationals, and VEX AI Worlds (June 12–14, UC Berkeley) as part of Team 3303X.


Beyond robotics, I care about people. I lead the alcohol-awareness team at CourAGE, Contra Costa County's youth addiction prevention program — motivated by losing my grandfather to tobacco. I'm a Life Scout working toward Eagle and a first-generation Indian-American.


North star: build robots that help humanity live on other planets.

Why I Build

"Build robots that help humanity reach further — in space, in healthcare, and in communities technology has left behind."

ReachBot · Independence for Seniors

After six years building robots to win trophies, I started thinking about who robots actually serve. ReachBot is my answer: a sub-$300 voice-controlled arm for seniors. Real engineering, real fabrication — not a concept.

AI for Healthcare Equity

My grandfather died from tobacco-induced throat cancer. That drives my work at CourAGE and my proposal for AI-powered early detection of substance abuse disorders, prioritized for underserved communities.

Aerospace + Robotics

Kennedy Space Center crystallized it: Mars rovers prove robots can go where humans can't. My goal is designing robots for the Artemis generation. VEX is the foundation. Planetary exploration is the destination.

Teaching as Engineering

As 3303J leader I never just solve problems — I teach others to solve them. Whether explaining PID to teammates or mentoring VEX IQ kids, real understanding means explaining the why, not just the how.

Engineering

Design & Calculations

Build Phase Roadmap
Design Iteration Flow
4 Design Iterations
March 2026 · Iter 01
Lego Hand — Dual Paddle
Flat PLA paddles, single MG996R, 32–200mm span. Too industrial for senior care context.
Abandoned — poor compliance on irregular objects
March 2026 · Iter 02
Claw — Fin Ray Geometry
Curved surfaces for better conforming. Still looked threatening to elderly users.
Designed, not built — backup retained
April 2026 · Iter 03
Octopus Arms — Soft Robotics
6 TPU tentacles, pneumatic actuation. Fails on flat surfaces; needs extruder unavailable at school.
Rejected — incompatible with school printer
April 2026 · Iter 04 — Current
ORCA Hand Mod — 5-Digit Human Hand
ETH Zurich ORCA v1 CAD, modified to 1 MG996R via unified tendon. 17 motors → 1 servo. $2,200 → $90.
● Phase 1 in active fabrication
Engineering Calculations
Calc 01 — Shoulder Torque
T = F × d = (0.25 kg × 9.81) × 0.76m = 1.86 N·m = 19.0 kg·cm MG996R: 11 kg·cm @ 6V ✓ (gripper) Phase 2: DS3225 @ 25 kg·cm
Adequate for gripper; Phase 2 needs upgrade
Calc 02 — Arm Length
arm ≥ 650mm + 110mm = 760mm
Final arm tube: 760mm (2.5ft)
Calc 03 — Safety Factor
Al 6061-T6 yield: 276 MPa σ = 1.86 N·m / 560mm³ ≈ 3.3 MPa
Safety factor > 80× — well within yield
Calc 04 — Floor Reach
reach = 760mm × sin(52°) = 599mm clearance = 720 − 599 = 121mm ✓
Gripper reaches floor with 121mm margin
